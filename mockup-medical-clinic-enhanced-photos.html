What is Frozen Shoulder?

Adhesive capsulitis, commonly known as frozen shoulder, is a condition where the shoulder joint capsule becomes inflamed and stiff, causing pain and a progressive loss of mobility.
Typical symptoms include persistent pain, pronounced stiffness, and difficulty moving the shoulder. Without proper treatment, this condition can severely impair daily activities and may result in long-term disability.
Early, specialized intervention—such as the MCD procedure—can prevent chronic pain and restore shoulder function.

The MCD Procedure – Non-Surgical Frozen Shoulder Cure

The Manual Capsular Dissection (MCD) procedure is a breakthrough, non-surgical treatment with a 98% success rate.
Unlike conventional methods, MCD rapidly isolates and liberates scar tissue with precise manual techniques, restoring motion and resolving pain.
